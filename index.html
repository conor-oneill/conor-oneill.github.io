Latest Project — Linear Regression via Gradient Descent

Using gradient descent to perform linear regression.

12/01/16

Linear regression is one of the most common and straight-forward methods of modelling observational and experimental data. It is used extensively throughout a variety of fields such as science, economics, finance, and pretty much anything where someone is interested in predictive modelling (though not extrapolation) or error reduction. A simple example might be modelling how the height of a person is related to their weight. A slightly more involved example might be modelling housing prices based on their size, number of bedrooms, locality, age, sale year, etc. Note that despite the name "linear," functions other than straight lines can be fit to data, such as n-degree polynomials.
